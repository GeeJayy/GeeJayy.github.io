Computational Modeling of Swelling Porous Systems

Multi-dimensional effects of viscoelastic relaxation in asymmetrical swelling porous media. Many biological, industrial, and geophysical problems involve swelling and shrinking porous bodies. Among these are biological tissues, drug delivery substrates, fabrics, and geophysical bodies such as soils and shales, to name a few. Over the last quarter century, rigorous field equations for conservation of mass and energy, and balances of linear and angular momentum have been developed for swelling systems with constitutive models which are consistent with the 2nd law of thermodynamics. Under limiting conditions, these models can be reduced to a system of non-local PDE (i.e. integro-partial differential equations) that display viscoelastic character. Here we reformulate one of the models in terms of a system of ODE’s, which are solved in a multidimensional finite element setting with a deforming grid via Comsol Multiphysics and Matlab computational software. Results presented are for an initially spherically symmetric body, which undergoes asymmetrical swelling and shrinking deformation due to asymmetric boundary conditions.
